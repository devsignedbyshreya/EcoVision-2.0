What are Sustainable Development Goals?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a collection of 17 global goals established by the United Nations to address the world's most pressing challenges. These include poverty, hunger, health, education, climate change, inequality, clean energy, sustainable cities, and global partnerships.

The SDGs provide a shared framework for governments, organizations, and individuals to work towards a better and more sustainable future for all.

Project Objectives

This project aims to automate the classification of news articles under the 17 Sustainable Development Goals using advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) and deep learning techniques.

• Analyze the textual content of sustainability-related news articles
• Identify the most relevant SDG categories (1–17) through multi-label classification
• Support multilingual news processing using language detection and translation
• Perform sentiment analysis to classify articles as Positive, Negative, or Neutral
• Provide fast, scalable, and accurate AI-driven classification
• Enable contextual and semantic understanding using DistilBERT
• Generate structured insights from large volumes of unstructured news data
• Support researchers, NGOs, ESG platforms, and policymakers with sustainability intelligence
• Enable real-time prediction and interactive visualization through a dashboard interface
• Facilitate large-scale sustainability monitoring and trend analysis
• Enable structured access to SDG-related information and sustainability insights

By combining Machine learning and LLM based insights, the system helps bridge the gap between unstructured news data and meaningful global development insights.
